STATEMENT OF ETHICS

Introduction

Our project is an autonomously driving NEATO that traverses along a track we have constructed. In today's world, the autonomous vehicle industry is rapidly developing with several companies like Tesla, BMW and Honda working towards making cars increasingly autonomous so that they can be completely hands-free. As we come closer to a future where autonomous vehicles are more commonplace, it is important to consider some of the ethical implications of autonomous driving both within our project and in the real world. We have outlined some of the potential negative impacts of autonomous cars below along with possible methods of mitigation.

Potential Negative Societal Impact 1

Self-driving cars are capable of directly facilitating injury to living beings. An important ethical consideration that arises from this is the question of who is responsible when they fail and cause injury.

Methods of Mitigation

Methods of mitigation: We can look to Waymo, a leading autonomous driving technology company, and their safety framework that increases transparency and accountability. This framework includes Safety Methodologies and Safety Readiness Determinations and discusses how they embed safety in all aspects of their technological and product development. Having their safety considerations comprehensively documented in this way allows for experts and academia to evaluate their performance and makes it easier to identify points of weakness when the system does fail.

Potential Negative Societal Impact 2

Containing information that could be deduced about individuals that they have not consented to share. Self-driving cars have an expansive sensory suite that can be used for surveillance or enable law enforcement to more aggressively overpolice minority areas. Also, self-driving cars could potentially talk to each other in future to navigate traffic more efficiently, which would require data-sharing.

Potential Negative Societal Impact 3

Unemployment of industries that rely on a human driver, such as taxi, share ride, trucking services, etc

Methods of Mitigation

Methods of mitigation: To protect populations whose livelihood may be replaced by self-driving cars, we can leverage existing unions or establish new unions that focus on the rights of drivers. If a transition to self-driving cars is inevitable, guidelines should be introduced to ensure self-driving cars are introduced at a slower pace, such that the replaced human drivers will have time to find a new job and their lives are not disturbed too much.

Potential Negative Societal Impact 4

Studies have shown that self-driving cars will lead to greater carbon emissions.

Methods of Mitigation

Methods of mitigation: Requiring future cars to use zero emission technology is one way that the effects of this can be limited. Adding a price onto driving is another way to regulate usage - one regulation could be to add fees for miles driven per car. Another option would be to promote shared usage of fleets of automated cars instead of private ownership, similar to Lyft.
